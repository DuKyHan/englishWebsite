Comparative Forms of Adjectives & Adverbs

📍 Comparative Adjectives

SHORT ADJECTIVES (1 syllable)

Structure: S + be + adjective + -er + than + S

Examples: bigger, smaller, taller, faster, newer

LONG ADJECTIVES (2+ syllables)

Structure: S + be + more/less + adjective + than + S

Examples: more expensive, more beautiful, more interesting

📊 Rules for Adding -er

Rule Example Comparative
Most adjectives + -er tall, fast taller, faster
Ends with 'e' + -r nice, large nicer, larger
CVC (consonant-vowel-consonant) → double consonant + -er big, hot, fat bigger, hotter, fatter
Ends with 'y' → change to 'i' + -er happy, pretty happier, prettier

⚠️ Irregular Adjectives

Adjective Comparative
good better
bad worse
far farther / further
much / many more
little less
old older / elder
📍 Comparative Adverbs

SHORT ADVERBS (1 syllable)

Structure: S + V + adverb + -er + than

Examples: harder, faster, earlier, better

LONG ADVERBS (usually ending in -ly)

Structure: S + V + more/less + adverb + than

Examples: more carefully, more slowly, more beautifully

⚡ Example Sentences

Comparative Adjectives:
  • ✓ China is bigger than Japan.
  • ✓ This book is more interesting than that one.
  • ✓ She is happier than usual.
  • ✓ Living in the countryside is more peaceful than the city.
Comparative Adverbs:
  • ✓ She runs faster than me.
  • ✓ He speaks English more fluently than his brother.
  • ✓ My sister cooks better than I do.
  • ✓ We need to work harder than before.
